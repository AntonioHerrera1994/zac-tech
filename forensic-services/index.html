Capabilities 5 Analytical Methods

Core Analytical
Capabilities

Five complementary analytical techniques — from surface imaging to deep structural inspection — covering every scale and material type your investigation requires.

Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)
High-resolution imaging for surface and microstructural analysis of materials — resolving features down to the nanometer scale.
Applications
  • Micro-cracks and fracture analysis
  • Surface contamination identification
  • Metallization and interconnect inspection
  • Morphology of advanced materials
Energy Dispersive X-ray (EDX)
Elemental composition analysis directly coupled with SEM imaging — mapping atomic distribution across any region of interest.
Applications
  • Contaminant identification
  • Failure root cause analysis
  • Material phase characterization
  • Foreign particle identification
Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)
Ultra-high resolution analysis of internal structures at the nanoscale — imaging through material cross-sections with atomic-level detail.
Applications
  • Crystal defect analysis
  • Interface and layer inspection
  • Nanostructure characterization
  • Advanced semiconductor device analysis
X-Ray Imaging & Diffraction (2D / 3D)
Non-destructive internal inspection and structural analysis — penetrating packaging without altering the sample.
Capabilities
  • 2D and 3D internal inspection
  • X-Ray diffraction — crystallographic analysis
  • Defect detection in multilayer devices
  • Porosity, voids, and delamination analysis
C-SAM — Scanning Acoustic Microscopy
Advanced acoustic inspection for non-destructive internal analysis — imaging material interfaces layer by layer using high-frequency ultrasound.
Applications
  • Delamination detection
  • Crack propagation mapping
  • Void and interface defect detection
  • Packaging integrity evaluation
