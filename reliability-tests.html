HTOL 01 / 07
High Temperature Operating Life

Stress Testing at
Elevated Temps

This test is used to determine the effects of bias conditions and temperature on solid-state devices over time. It simulates the operating status of devices in an accelerated manner, exposing latent defects and failure mechanisms that would otherwise only appear after years of field operation.

LTOL 02 / 07
Low Temperature Operating Life

Reliability Under
Extreme Cold

A reliability test similar to HTOL, but instead of applying high temperature, low temperature (e.g. -40°C or -55°C) is applied while the device is electrically operating. This test reveals failure modes unique to cold-environment semiconductor behavior and ensures performance in automotive and industrial applications.

ELFR 03 / 07
Early Life Failure Rate of Integrated Circuits

Detecting Failures
Before They Happen

This test provides failure characteristics early in the life of new or untested technologies when generic data is not available. Essential for qualifying cutting-edge ICs entering mass production, ELFR identifies early-life failure modes and establishes baseline reliability data for your production line.

RF HTOL 04 / 07
High Temperature Life at Radio Frequency

RF Component
Life Qualification

This test determines the effects of RF bias conditions and temperature on power amplifier modules (PAMs), switches, transceivers, tuners, and other RF components over time. It simulates the operating status of devices in an accelerated manner — critical for the 5G and wireless communications supply chain.

PTC 05 / 07
Temperature and Power Cycles

Withstanding
Thermal Extremes

This test is performed to determine the ability of a device to withstand alternating exposures to extreme high and low temperatures with operational biases periodically applied and removed. It validates the mechanical and electrical integrity of semiconductor packages under real-world power cycling conditions.

THB 06 / 07
Temperature and Humidity Bias Test

Humidity & Bias
Life Validation

This standard establishes a method and defined conditions for performing a temperature and humidity shelf life test with applied bias. It evaluates the susceptibility of semiconductor packages to moisture-induced degradation and corrosion under active electrical conditions — critical for outdoor and high-humidity applications.

HAST 07 / 07
Highly Accelerated Stress Test

Humidity Reliability
at Extreme Speed

The purpose of this test method is to evaluate the reliability of non-hermetically packaged solid-state devices in humid environments. Using supersaturated steam at pressures above atmospheric, HAST achieves in hours what standard HTHB tests take weeks to complete — without sacrificing result accuracy or reproducibility.
