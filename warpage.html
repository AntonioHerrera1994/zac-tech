The Challenge

When AI Chips Run Hot,
Packages Fail

Modern AI accelerators — systems like SambaNova's multi-die GPU packages — integrate multiple processor dies and HBM memory stacks onto a single organic substrate running at 2,500 to 5,000 Watts of continuous thermal load.

The substrate is the green board that holds everything together. It is composed of organic resins (ABF — Ajinomoto Build-up Film) reinforced with glass fiber. At extreme temperatures, the organic material's Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) — around 15 ppm/°C — differs dramatically from the silicon die's CTE of ~2.6 ppm/°C.

That mismatch forces the package to bow, twist, and deform with every thermal cycle. In large flip-chip BGA packages, a bare die can exceed 300µm (12 mil) of warpage — well beyond JEDEC's 200µm tolerance limit for high-reliability assemblies.

The industry is converging on glass substrates as a replacement, offering dramatically lower CTE mismatch, superior flatness, and finer redistribution layer routing. But both organic and glass packages must be characterized before market release.

Our Methodology

Three-Phase
Characterization Protocol

01
Phase One

Extreme Environment Stress Testing

Before any optical or acoustic inspection, we subject the package to the harshest conditions it will realistically face — and then some. The goal is to induce the maximum possible mechanical and thermal damage in a controlled environment, replicating full board-assembly soldering and field operation cycles. This reveals hidden failure modes before the product ships.

Thermal Cycling
Repeated exposure between extreme cold and heat (e.g., -40°C to +150°C per IPC-9701 / JEDEC standards), cycling dozens to hundreds of times to fatigue solder joints and expose CTE-mismatch failures.
Reflow Simulation
Simulates the solder reflow process used during board assembly — heating the package through a SAC305 lead-free profile up to 260°C — to measure real-time warpage at its most critical moment.
High-Temp Soak
Sustained dwell at operating temperature to evaluate creep, viscoelastic relaxation, and long-term package stability under conditions that mirror active AI accelerator deployment.
02
Phase Two

Planarity and Structural Analysis

After stress conditioning, we deploy full-field non-contact optical measurement and acoustic microscopy to build a complete picture of the package's physical state — both surface topology and internal structural integrity.

Shadow Moire
Full-field non-contact optical technique per JEDEC JESD22-B112. A collimated light source through a Ronchi grating creates interference fringe maps — contour plots of out-of-plane displacement across the entire package surface in real time, including at reflow temperatures up to 260°C.
CSAM Acoustic Analysis
C-Mode Scanning Acoustic Microscopy uses high-frequency ultrasound to image the interior of the package layer by layer. Detects delamination, voids, cracks, and underfill separation that are invisible to optical inspection.
FEM Correlation / DIC
Measured warpage and strain data is correlated against Finite Element Models. Digital Image Correlation (DIC) is used to capture full-field displacement and validate or refine simulation models for next-generation package design.

Phase Three

Characterization Report and Findings

All measurement data, acoustic scans, and thermal profiles are consolidated into a structured engineering report. The deliverable gives your design and reliability teams a clear, actionable picture of package performance — and any failures detected.

Warpage Curve Report

Reliability Assessment
Engineering conclusions on solder joint reliability, expected thermal fatigue life, and go/no-go recommendation with pass/fail criteria aligned to your product spec.

Technical Methods

Measurement Capabilities

Shadow Moire Interferometry
JEDEC JESD22-B112 — Full-Field Optical Planarity

Non-contact, full-field technique that creates contour maps of surface out-of-plane displacement across the entire package. The interference between the reference grating and its shadow on the sample generates fringe patterns — each fringe represents a fixed height interval (typically pitch/2 per fringe).

Phase-stepping increases resolution by laterally shifting the grating in sub-pitch increments, enabling sub-micron height sensitivity across large substrate panels. Measurement occurs live through the entire thermal profile.

C-SAM Acoustic Microscopy
C-Mode Scanning Acoustic Microscopy — Internal Defect Imaging

High-frequency ultrasonic pulses (typically 15–230 MHz) are focused into the package. Reflections from material boundaries — especially air gaps — produce C-mode images of every internal interface: die-to-underfill, underfill-to-substrate, inter-laminar layers.

Delamination, voids, cracks, and moisture-induced damage appear as bright contrast regions. Scanning is performed layer by layer to produce a complete internal defect map before and after stress conditioning.
