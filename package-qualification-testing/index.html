What is a
Semiconductor
Package?

A semiconductor package is the protective enclosure around a silicon chip. Its main purpose is to shield the chip and its delicate internal connections from environmental stress such as humidity, temperature fluctuations, chemicals, and mechanical pressure.

In addition to protection, the package connects the chip to the outside world, allowing it to interface with other components or systems through pins, leads, or solder balls (BGA). Essentially, the package ensures that the semiconductor functions reliably while safely integrating into electronic devices.

What We Test

Package Types
We Qualify

Our laboratory handles the full spectrum of modern semiconductor package formats — from legacy through-hole designs to advanced 3D-stacked configurations used in cutting-edge applications.

BGA
Ball Grid Array
High-density I/O packaging used in processors and FPGAs. We evaluate solder ball integrity, warpage, and board-level drop/shock resistance.
QFP
Quad Flat Package
Surface-mount package with leads on all four sides. Qualification includes lead co-planarity, solderability, and thermal fatigue cycling.
WLP
Wafer-Level Package
Advanced packaging where the chip is packaged at wafer scale. Critical tests include dicing stress, HAST, and board-level reliability under thermal shock.
LGA
Land Grid Array
Contact-pad based packages common in RF and power modules. Testing covers contact resistance, mechanical durability, and humidity exposure.
SIP
System in Package
Multi-die integrated packages demanding comprehensive qualification: inter-die thermal stress, EMI shielding, and moisture-sensitivity levels (MSL).
3D-IC
3D-Stacked IC
Chiplet-based stacked packages with TSV interconnects. Our protocols address inter-layer delamination, thermal resistance, and electromigration under high current density.
Our Methodology

How Package
Qualification Works

Every qualification engagement follows a structured four-phase protocol aligned with JEDEC JESD22, AEC-Q100/Q101, and customer-specific acceptance criteria. From initial sample receipt through final data delivery, each step is documented and traceable.

01
Sample Intake & Preconditioning
Incoming samples are logged, inspected under optical and X-ray microscopy, then subjected to MSL preconditioning (reflow simulation) to replicate the board-assembly environment before stress testing begins.
02
Environmental Stress Testing
Accelerated protocols including Thermal Cycling (TC), Highly Accelerated Stress Test (HAST), Temperature-Humidity-Bias (THB), and Unbiased HAST expose packages to worst-case field conditions in compressed timeframes.
03
Mechanical & Board-Level Evaluation
Solder joint reliability is assessed via thermal cycling on board (CoB), drop/shock testing, and vibration. Warpage and co-planarity measurements ensure assembly compatibility with automated pick-and-place lines.
04
Analysis & Reporting
Post-stress electrical parametric testing isolates any degradation. SEM and cross-section analysis identify failure modes. A comprehensive engineering report with Weibull statistics and corrective action recommendations is delivered.
