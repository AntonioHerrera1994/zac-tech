Discharge Models:
Human & Machine

Component-level ESD tests simulate real-world electrostatic events during manufacturing, handling, and operation — establishing the voltage thresholds at which device damage begins.

HBM 01 / 05
Human Body Model

When Human Contact
Becomes the Threat

Simulates the electrostatic discharge that can occur when a person carrying an electric charge touches a device. It is one of the most common tests used to determine component sensitivity to human handling — a critical qualification gate for consumer electronics, industrial ICs, and automotive semiconductors exposed to frequent manual contact.

JEDEC JS-001 · JESD22-A114 · AEC-Q100-002

MM 02 / 05
Machine Model

Automated Lines,
Invisible Discharges

Replicates discharges that may happen through automated equipment, handling tools, or mechanical interfaces, evaluating the potential impact on electronic components in industrial environments. With no human resistance in the discharge path, MM events are faster and more energetic — making them particularly damaging to fine-geometry device nodes.

System & Device Level 03–04 / 05

Charged Device &
System Response

Beyond component-level testing, real-world ESD events occur at the device and system level — during automated production and after full board assembly in end-user environments.

S-ESD 03 / 05
System-Level Electrostatic Discharge

Full-System ESD
Immunity Validation

Simulates ESD events after the full assembly of a system or circuit board, evaluating the response of the complete unit to external discharges typical of end-user environments. System-level ESD per IEC 61000-4-2 applies voltages up to ±8kV contact / ±15kV air — conditions that board-level protection circuits, TVS diodes, and layout strategies must collectively withstand.

CDM 04 / 05
Charged Device Model

When the Device Itself
Becomes the Source

Assesses the susceptibility when the device itself becomes electrostatically charged due to friction or induction, discharging it upon contact with grounded surfaces. This is especially relevant in automated manufacturing processes — CDM events are the fastest of all ESD models (<1ns rise time) and responsible for the majority of ESD failures in modern automated SMT production lines.

LU 05 / 05
Latch-Up Protection Testing

Overvoltage Resistance.
Parasitic Structures.
No Margin for Error.

This test verifies the resistance of integrated circuits to overvoltage conditions that activate internal parasitic structures (like thyristors), causing a sustained current flow that can permanently damage the device. Latch-up represents a catastrophic failure mode — once triggered, the device draws excessive current and cannot self-recover without power cycling. Critical for CMOS ICs in automotive, industrial, and high-reliability applications.

JEDEC JESD78 · AEC-Q100-004 · IEC 60749-34
