Optical and Digital Microscopy

We analyze surfaces at high magnification to reveal microdefects invisible to the naked eye. High-resolution optical systems resolve surface cracks, contamination, bonding anomalies, and metal layer irregularities across the entire component area.

Infrared Thermography

Chemical or Mechanical Decapsulation

We remove the package to expose the silicon die. Precise chemical or plasma-assisted decapsulation techniques grant access to the die surface, bond wires, and interconnect layers — enabling direct inspection of structures impossible to reach non-destructively.

Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)

We study microscopic details such as fractures in tracks, metal migration, or contamination. Sub-micron resolution SEM imaging resolves failure sites at the transistor level — providing irrefutable evidence of crack propagation paths, electromigration voids, and dielectric breakdown mechanisms.

Get Your Quote

Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy (EDS/EDX)

We determine the chemical composition of residues or contaminants that caused the failure. Element mapping integrated with SEM identifies foreign material, oxide layers, ionic contamination, and alloy phase changes at the exact failure site with full elemental attribution.
